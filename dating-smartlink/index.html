01 / Operational context

A SmartLink is a decision layer

The SmartLink is not the traffic source and it is not the final dating product. It is the routing layer between them. Its job is to interpret the click context, apply eligibility rules and choose an available route.

02 / Operational context

Routing starts with eligibility

A destination must first support the user GEO, device and other required conditions. Only then can performance data influence the choice. This distinction prevents a high-performing but ineligible offer from receiving the wrong traffic.

03 / Operational context

Performance feedback improves future decisions

Conversions, statuses and payout values create a feedback loop. Reliable postbacks and source identifiers are therefore part of the product, not an optional reporting detail.

Launch checklist

What to validate before volume increases.

Pass a unique click identifier
Use subIDs for source and placement
Do not mix incompatible traffic in one unlabelled link
Confirm postback status and payout fields
Compare approved results, not only raw registrations
Keep fallback coverage active for mixed GEO traffic
Operational principle

SmartLink routing does not replace transparent traffic acquisition, stable tracking or partner communication. It makes those inputs more useful by connecting them to eligible dating inventory.
